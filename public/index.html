Progression, Deload + Injury Prevention
Load Progression Rules
CategoryRuleNotes
Strength (compound)Add 2.5–5 kg after 2 consecutive sessions with clean formNever add because the calendar says so
Accessory + UnilateralIncrease reps first (8→12), then weight (drop back to 8)Double progression model
PlyometricsAdd 1 rep per set per week max · Never add height/distance and reps togetherQuality of landing > quantity
Carry + Grip+10% distance or weight per week — not bothTendon adaptation is slower than muscle
Running volumeNever increase weekly mileage more than 10% per weekMost running injuries are overuse
Elbow Protection — Non-Negotiable Rules
RuleDetail
Never lock elbows under loadKeep 5–10° flexion at full extension on all pressing movements
Wrist prep before every upper sessionLoaded wrist flexion/extension — never skip
Tyler Twist daily3 sets every training day, light band, slow eccentric — clinical prehab standard
If elbow pain startsDrop direct bicep/tricep work. Switch to landmine press. Neutral grip for pulls. Keep carries for blood flow.
Deload — Every 4th Week
ElementDeload approach
Weights50% of normal load · Same sets and reps · Use deload week to try new exercise options from the ranked lists
PlyometricsRemove entirely on deload week — replace with pogo hops or light skipping only
Mobility + BreathworkKeep at full volume — these are the weeks you make the most mobility gains
RunningZone 2 only, no intervals or tempo on deload week
Early deload signalsJoint pain, sleep disruption, motivation loss, 2+ weeks of declining performance, persistent fatigue
